Profile

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at São Paulo State University (UNESP), Institute of Geosciences and Exact Sciences (IGCE), Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics (DEMAC).

I completed my Ph.D. at the National Institute for Space Research (INPE) (2022–2025) in the Graduate Program in Space Engineering and Technology. During my doctoral studies, I spent one year (2023–2024) as a Visiting Researcher at the Department of Planetary Science of the German Aerospace Center (DLR – Deutsches Zentrum für Luft- und Raumfahrt), in Berlin, Germany.

I hold a Master’s degree in Physics (Applied Physics) from São Paulo State University (UNESP), Rio Claro Campus (2020–2022), and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics Education from the Federal University of São Carlos (UFSCar), Araras Campus (2015–2019).

My research experience includes Orbital Dynamics with emphasis on retrograde orbits in planetary systems, orbital transfers, and machine learning applications. I also have experience in computational simulations of molecular optical properties and physics teaching instrumentation.

My current research interests include Celestial Mechanics, Astrodynamics, and Machine Learning.
