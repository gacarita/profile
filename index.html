Profile

Currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at São Paulo State University (UNESP), Institute of Geosciences and Exact Sciences (IGCE), Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics (DEMAC).

He completed his Ph.D. at the National Institute for Space Research (INPE) (2022–2025) in the Graduate Program in Space Engineering and Technology. During his doctoral studies, he spent one year (2023–2024) as a Visiting Researcher at the Department of Planetary Science of the German Aerospace Center (DLR – Deutsches Zentrum für Luft- und Raumfahrt), Berlin, Germany.

He holds a Master’s degree in Physics (Applied Physics) from São Paulo State University (UNESP), Rio Claro Campus (2020–2022), and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics Education from the Federal University of São Carlos (UFSCar), Araras Campus (2015–2019).

His research experience includes Orbital Dynamics, with emphasis on retrograde orbits in planetary systems, orbital transfers, and machine learning applications. Additional experience includes computational simulations of molecular optical properties, physics teaching instrumentation, and applied machine learning.

Current research interests include Celestial Mechanics, Astrodynamics, and Machine Learning.
